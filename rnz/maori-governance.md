# Māori And Multilingual Governance Gate

This gate applies before publishing corrected, translated, language-labelled, or authoritative entity-linked derivatives.

- Canonical audio and transcript outputs remain unchanged.
- Corrections preserve the original text, word or segment offsets, resource version, confidence and reviewer disposition.
- Lexical resources must identify their source, licence or permission basis, version and maintainer.
- Language signals are review prompts, not claims about identity, ethnicity or speaker affiliation.
- Translation is opt-in and must record target language, model or translator, revision, confidence and provenance.
- Māori speech, names, iwi, hapū, places and culturally sensitive material require Māori-informed review before promotion.
- Correction, takedown and access decisions require an authenticated operator disposition; no model output performs them automatically.
- Until these fields are complete, derivatives remain review-only and canonical archival continues independently.
